"Medicinal" Wildlife Trade

Secreted by the liver, bile is pumped from the gallbladder of various species of bears and has been used in traditional Chinese "medicine" since at least the 8th century. Diseases are common on bear bile farms, bile from sick animals can be contaiminated with blood, feces, pus, urine, and bacteria that may threaten human health.

Factory Farming

Over 150 million pigs are raised in factory farms and killed for food each year. Pigs are very intelligent with cognitive abilities comparable to dogs and some primates. Though pigs can live upward of 10 years, most are sent to slaughter at around 4 or 5 months old.
Captive Orcas

Wild Orcas, or Killer Whales, can live up to 50 or even 80 years, but when they are held captive in ocean amusement parks such as Seaworld, most of them don't live past 30. There are close to 60 orcas in capitivity around the world and there have been numerous incidents, including deaths of orca trainers because these majestic creatures are simply not suited to live in confined spaces when their natural home is the vast ocean.
Dog Meat Trade

Contaminated Water Supplies

The Flint Water Crisis in Flint, Michigan, has been ongoing since 2014 when the city switched their drinking water supply from Detroit's system to the Flint River to save on costs. Inadequate treatment and testing of water resulted in health issues including skin rashes, hair loss, and itchy skin. Studies also revealed the contaminated water constributed to elevated blood lead levels. High levels of lead is especially harmful when exposed to infants, leading to impairment in brain development, reduction in IQ and physical growth, and it also contributes to anemia, hearing loss, cardiovascular disease, and behavioral problems.
